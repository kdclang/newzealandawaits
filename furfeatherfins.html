Fur, Feather and Fins
January 31 - February 14, 2016

If you love all things nature, this trip is for you! You’ll experience fur seals, dolphins, sea and shore birds (including penguins, gannets, albatross, white heron, royal spoonbill, godwit), and forest birds (hihi/stitchbird, takahe, tieke/saddleback, kaka, weka to name few). Having evolved without the mammalian predators present in other countries, our birdlife is truly unique. We’ll visit eco-sanctuaries (link to wilderness page for more info) where you’ll be inspired by the efforts of countless volunteers who are working to protect our endangered native species and forests from introduced predators, restoring the forest ecosystems to their pre-human state. You’ll experience Maori culture and you’ll also meet our prehistoric lizard, the tuatara. We’ll take in the iconic scenery that New Zealand is famous for, including geothermal regions, lush rainforests, glacial and alpine vistas, and a remote wilderness fiord. We’ll take you to places that most New Zealanders haven’t yet experienced. To fully enjoy this trip, a moderate degree of fitness is required.

If you love the buzz and frenzy of cities, then this tour is not for you as the majority of our nights will be in quiet, rural lodging. We’ll also spend several nights in B&B's providing you with an authentic view into life in New Zealand as you share the homes of locals for the night.
